Self-Defense Quick Guides

30-second techniques for real emergency scenarios. Practice these regularly so they become instinct.

Remember: Your safety comes first. Running away is ALWAYS the best option. Use these only as last resort when escape is not possible.
COMMON SCENARIO
Someone Grabs Your Wrist

How to break free in 2 seconds

1 Rotate toward the thumb

The thumb is the weakest point of any grip. Pull your arm toward their thumb side — NOT away from their fingers.

2 Yank sharply and fast

Don't pull slowly — one quick, sharp jerk breaks the grip. Use your entire body weight, not just arm strength.

3 Step back immediately

Create distance. Turn and RUN. Scream "FIRE" (people respond faster to fire than help).

Pro tip: If both wrists are grabbed, clasp your hands together, then push UP and OUT through the gap between their arms.
HIGH DANGER
Grabbed from Behind (Bear Hug)

Break free from a rear hold

1 Drop your weight DOWN

Bend your knees and drop suddenly. This makes you heavier and harder to hold. Widen your stance.

2 Stomp on their foot HARD

Use your heel on their instep. The small bones in feet cause extreme pain when stomped.

3 Elbow strike to ribs/stomach

Drive your elbow backward into their solar plexus (center of stomach). Repeat rapidly.

4 Head-butt backward

Slam the back of your head into their nose/face. Then RUN.

MOST COMMON
Being Followed on the Street

What to do before it escalates

1 Confirm you're being followed

Cross the street. If they cross too, make another turn. Three turns in the same direction = confirmation.

2 Enter a populated place

Walk into any shop, restaurant, mosque, or petrol station. Tell the staff someone is following you.

3 Call someone and SPEAK LOUDLY

Call anyone — describe the person following you out loud: "Han Abu, ek banda mere peeche aa raha hai, neeli shirt pehni hai..."

4 NEVER go home

Do NOT lead them to your house. Go to a police station, hospital, or any 24/7 public area.

PAKISTAN SPECIFIC
Unsafe Situation in Taxi/Rickshaw

If the driver takes wrong route or behaves inappropriately

1 Speak loudly on phone

"Bhai mera driver galat raste pe jaa raha hai. Number plate [READ IT OUT]. Main is waqt [LOCATION] pe hun."

2 Demand to stop at a busy area

"Yahan rokein" — point at any shop, petrol pump, or traffic signal with people around.

3 Create a scene if ignored

Start honking from back seat, bang on windows to attract attention of other vehicles. Scream if needed.

4 Exit at any red light/stop

When vehicle slows at a signal, open door and exit quickly. Don't worry about fare — your safety matters more.

Prevention: Always log vehicle details in SafePath's Transport Verify feature BEFORE getting in. Share plate number with contacts.
LAHORE/KARACHI
Phone/Bag Snatching Attempt

What to do during and after

1 LET IT GO if they have a weapon

No phone is worth your life. If they show a knife or gun, give the item immediately. Do NOT resist.

2 If no weapon: grip and scream

Hold the strap with both hands close to your body. Scream "CHOR! CHOR!" as loud as possible.

3 After snatching: remember details

Bike color, number plate (even partial), direction they went, how many people, clothing. Note IMMEDIATELY.

4 Report within 1 hour

Call 15 or go to nearest police station. CCTV footage is overwritten quickly — fast reporting matters.

SELF DEFENSE
Vulnerable Pressure Points

Where to strike for maximum effect

Target these areas to create pain and escape. Strike HARD and FAST, then RUN:

👁️ Eyes
Poke/scratch — causes temporary blindness
👃 Nose
Palm strike upward — extreme pain + tears
🦵 Groin
Knee strike — incapacitates immediately
🦶 Instep
Stomp hard — small bones break easily
👂 Ears
Slap both ears hard — causes disorientation
🦴 Shin
Kick with shoe edge — very painful
Key principle: SING method — Solar plexus, Instep, Nose, Groin. Strike in that order and RUN.
